# Research Harness

## 0. Original Title
Can Small Language Models Learn to Self-Correct Through Synthetic Critiques?

## 1. Paper Type
Research Paper

## 2. One-sentence Research Pitch
Test whether synthetic critiques from stronger models help 1B-3B language models improve answer revision on math reasoning, and whether that improvement reflects genuine error diagnosis rather than critique-style imitation.

## 3. Problem Statement
Small language models often fail to correct their own reasoning errors. Synthetic critique training may improve revisions, but the observed gains may come from supervised answer editing rather than true self-correction.

## 4. Why This Matters
If critique training only teaches surface-level revision, then claims about small-model self-correction are overstated. A clearer diagnosis would help researchers design better training and evaluation protocols.

## 5. Research Question
Can synthetic critique training teach small language models to identify and correct reasoning errors, beyond improving answer editing under critique-conditioned supervision?

## 6. Core Hypotheses
- H1: Synthetic critiques improve critique-conditioned answer revision on math reasoning.
- H2: Some of the gain transfers to unseen error types or OOD math tasks.
- H3: If the model cannot localize errors or resist misleading critiques, the self-correction claim is too strong.

## 7. Novelty Claim
The safer novelty is diagnostic: distinguish genuine error diagnosis from critique imitation in small-model self-correction training.

## 8. Related Work Buckets
- Self-correction and self-refinement in language models.
- Synthetic feedback and critique generation.
- Small language model finetuning.
- Verifier, reranking, and answer-only finetuning baselines.

## 9. Proposed Approach
Train 1B-3B models with synthetic critiques generated by a stronger model, then evaluate answer revision, error localization, OOD transfer, and robustness to corrupted critiques.

## 10. Minimum Viable Experiment
Run a small-scale GSM8K-style pilot comparing critique training against answer-only finetuning and verifier reranking.

## 11. Baselines
- Answer-only finetuning.
- Self-refine prompting without finetuning.
- Verifier reranking.
- Critique-free correction.

## 12. Ablations
- Remove critique text.
- Use corrupted or misleading critiques.
- Evaluate unseen error types.
- Vary model scale.

## 13. Evaluation Metrics
- Final answer accuracy.
- Revision success rate.
- Error localization accuracy.
- OOD transfer performance.
- Robustness to misleading critique.

## 14. Falsification Conditions
- Critique training matches answer-only finetuning with no diagnostic advantage.
- Gains disappear on unseen error types.
- The model follows misleading critiques without detecting contradictions.

## 15. Expected Contributions
- A diagnostic evaluation of synthetic critique training for small models.
- Evidence separating answer editing from stronger self-correction claims.
- Practical guidance on when critique training is or is not useful.

## 16. Known Risks
- The method may not beat simpler baselines.
- The self-correction claim may need to be narrowed.
- Synthetic critiques may introduce artifacts from the stronger model.

## 17. Assumptions and Unknowns
- Assumption: math reasoning is a suitable first testbed.
- Assumption: stronger-model critiques are available at sufficient quality.
- Unknown: whether error localization can be evaluated reliably at small scale.

## 18. Next 7-Day Plan
1. Define self-correction operationally.
2. Choose one math dataset and one OOD set.
3. Implement answer-only and critique-training baselines.
4. Create a small corrupted-critique diagnostic set.
5. Run a pilot and update the claim.

## 19. Questions for PI
1. Is diagnostic novelty enough, or does this need a stronger method contribution?
2. What baseline would most likely kill this idea?
3. How narrow should the self-correction claim be before experiments?
