# Research Harness

## 0. Original Title

Can Small Language Models Learn to Self-Correct Through Synthetic Critiques?

## 1. Paper Type

Research Paper

## 2. One-sentence Research Pitch

This project tests whether synthetic critiques from stronger models help 1B-3B open-source language models improve correction behavior on math reasoning, and whether that improvement depends causally on useful critique content rather than corrected-answer supervision or answer-revision templates.

## 3. Problem Statement

Small language models can revise incorrect answers when given additional supervision, but it is unclear whether synthetic critiques teach them to use error information or merely expose them to corrected outputs. The main risk is that the project claims "self-correction" while the evidence only supports ordinary answer revision.

## 4. Why This Matters

If critique content causally improves correction behavior, synthetic critiques could be a useful training signal for small models. If answer-only finetuning or corrected-answer-only supervision explains the gains, then strong claims about self-correction are misleading and the contribution should become a diagnostic empirical analysis.

## 5. Research Question

Do synthetic critiques provide value beyond corrected-answer supervision for improving correction behavior in small language models?

Sharper version:

Under what diagnostic conditions does critique content causally contribute to correcting initially wrong math reasoning answers?

## 6. Operational Definition

For this project, genuine self-correction means that a model:

- improves initially incorrect answers after receiving critique information;
- is sensitive to whether the critique is correct, generic, unrelated, or misleading;
- transfers at least partially to unseen error types;
- changes behavior when critique quality changes.

The following do not count as self-correction:

- memorizing answer-revision templates;
- blindly following critiques;
- improving only because corrected answers were included in training;
- exploiting GSM8K-specific patterns.

The safe operational label for early experiments is **critique-conditioned correction behavior**, not robust self-correction.

## 7. Core Hypotheses

- H1: Critique training improves correction behavior on initially wrong GSM8K answers.
- H2: Valid critiques provide value beyond answer-only finetuning and corrected-answer-only supervision.
- H3: The advantage of critique training shrinks or disappears when critiques are removed, generic, unrelated, or misleading.
- H4: If gains do not transfer to unseen error types, the result is likely template-specific answer rewriting rather than self-correction.

## 8. Novelty Claim

The novelty is diagnostic, not just methodological. The paper should not claim that training on synthetic critiques is new by itself.

The defensible novelty is:

This project separates critique-conditioned correction from corrected-answer supervision by testing whether model performance depends on the semantic content and correctness of critiques.

## 9. Related Work Buckets

- Self-correction and self-refinement in language models.
- Synthetic critique generation and AI feedback.
- Small language model finetuning.
- Answer-only finetuning and corrected-answer supervision.
- Verifier reranking and answer selection.
- Math reasoning evaluation, especially GSM8K-style tasks.
- Robustness to noisy, corrupted, or misleading feedback.

## 10. Proposed Approach

Construct training data with:

- question;
- incorrect answer;
- critique generated by a stronger model;
- corrected answer.

Train 1B-3B open-source language models under multiple conditions:

- answer-only finetuning;
- corrected-answer-only supervision;
- critique training with valid critiques.

Then evaluate whether correction behavior changes when critique content is removed, generic, unrelated, or misleading.

## 11. Minimum Viable Experiment

Task:

- GSM8K.

Training conditions:

1. Answer-only finetuning.
2. Corrected-answer-only supervision.
3. Critique training with valid critiques.

Ablations:

1. Critique removal.
2. Generic critique replacement.
3. Unrelated critique replacement.
4. Misleading critique.

Core diagnostic:

Performance should depend on critique content. Valid critiques should outperform answer-only supervision, while corrupted, unrelated, or misleading critiques should reduce that advantage.

## 12. Baselines

Primary baseline:

- Answer-only finetuning.

Secondary baselines:

- Corrected-answer-only supervision.
- Self-refine prompting.
- Verifier reranking.
- Base model without correction training.

The answer-only finetuning baseline is the most dangerous one. If it matches critique training, the strong self-correction claim should be dropped.

## 13. Ablations

- Remove critique text while preserving corrected answers.
- Replace critiques with generic feedback.
- Pair examples with unrelated critiques.
- Provide misleading critiques that point to the wrong error.
- Evaluate seen versus unseen error types.
- Optionally compare stronger-model critiques with weaker or noisier critiques.

## 14. Evaluation Metrics

- Initial answer accuracy.
- Post-correction accuracy.
- Correction success rate among initially wrong answers.
- Improvement over answer-only finetuning.
- Improvement over corrected-answer-only supervision.
- Sensitivity to critique correctness.
- Performance under unrelated or misleading critiques.
- Partial transfer to unseen error types.

Metric thresholds are not fixed yet and should be defined before the main experiment.

## 15. Falsification Conditions

Stop using the strong phrase "learn to self-correct" if:

- answer-only finetuning matches critique training;
- corrected-answer-only supervision explains the gains;
- critique removal or corruption has little effect;
- gains disappear on unseen error types;
- the model does not show sensitivity to critique correctness;
- the model blindly follows misleading critiques.

In those cases, reframe the paper as an empirical analysis of synthetic critique training rather than a self-correction paper.

## 16. Expected Contributions

- A diagnostic empirical study of synthetic critique training for small language models.
- Evidence on whether critique content provides value beyond corrected-answer supervision.
- A clearer operational distinction between self-correction, critique-conditioned correction, and answer rewriting.
- Practical guidance on when synthetic critiques are useful, redundant, or misleading.

## 17. Known Risks

- The title may still overclaim by using "self-correct."
- Answer-only finetuning may match critique training.
- GSM8K may be too narrow to support broad conclusions.
- Critiques from stronger models may leak corrected reasoning too directly.
- Misleading critique construction may be difficult to make fair and interpretable.
- The final contribution may be empirical analysis, not a method paper.

## 18. Assumptions and Unknowns

Assumptions:

- GSM8K is a reasonable first testbed.
- 1B-3B open-source models have enough capacity to learn from critique structure.
- Stronger-model critiques can be generated at usable quality.
- Critique corruption and misleading-critique conditions can be constructed cleanly.

Unknowns:

- Exact model families.
- Dataset size.
- Optimizer and training setup.
- Metric thresholds for "outperform," "shrink," and "partial transfer."
- How to define unseen error types robustly.

## 19. Next 7-Day Plan

1. Choose one primary 1B-3B model and one backup model.
2. Define metric thresholds for critique value before running the main experiment.
3. Build a small GSM8K pilot set with incorrect answers, valid critiques, and corrected answers.
4. Implement answer-only finetuning, corrected-answer-only supervision, and critique training.
5. Build the critique ablation set: removed, generic, unrelated, misleading.
6. Run a small pilot and compare whether valid critique content changes correction behavior.
7. Update the title and claim based on whether diagnostics support the strong framing.

## 20. Questions for PI

1. Should the title avoid "self-correct" until the diagnostic conditions pass?
2. Is answer-only finetuning the decisive baseline, or is verifier reranking more dangerous?
3. What threshold would be enough to claim critique content matters?
4. Is this better framed from the start as an empirical analysis paper rather than a method paper?
